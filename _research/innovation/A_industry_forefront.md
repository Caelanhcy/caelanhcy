# Innovation — Industry Forefront Briefing (2025–2026)

Working brief for the copywriter rewriting the opening of Caelan H.C.Y's essay on innovation. Goal: surface where senior practitioners are actually arguing right now so the rewrite resonates with sophisticated readers. Source dates noted; nothing older than 2024 unless used as historical anchor.

---

## 1. Current frameworks at the frontier

- **Aggregation Theory, post-AI revision** — Ben Thompson's original thesis (digital aggregators win by owning demand, marginal cost → zero) is being explicitly stress-tested. Doug O'Laughlin (Fabricated Knowledge, Jan 2025) argued reasoning models "ended" Aggregation Theory because compute reintroduces real marginal cost. Thompson's 2025 rebuttal: aggregation survives because *controlling demand still gives power over supply*, even when supply is expensive again. Why it matters: anyone using "platform economics" language pre-2024 sounds dated; the live debate is whether AI re-verticalizes the stack. Source: https://stratechery.com/2025/the-openai-critique-comparative-advantage-and-infrastructure-aggregation-theory-and-cheap-ai/ ; https://stratechery.com/2025/the-2025-stratechery-year-in-review/

- **Agentic Engineering (post–Vibe Coding)** — Karpathy coined "vibe coding" Feb 2025; by late 2025 / early 2026 he himself declared it passé and replaced it with "agentic engineering" — humans orchestrating coding agents with oversight, not writing code directly. Source: Karpathy via The New Stack, Nov 2025 (https://thenewstack.io/vibe-coding-is-passe/). Why it matters: "vibe coding" is already the tell of someone six months behind. Frontier framing is "Software 3.0 / agentic engineering."

- **AI-Native vs. Retrofit (Greenfield Strategy)** — a16z's "Greenfield Strategy" (2025) and "Notes on AI Apps in 2026" frame the contest as AI-native startups vs. incumbents retrofitting AI onto legacy stacks. Defensibility comes from being multi-model, owning cornered data resources, network effects, and ecosystem feature surface — not from the model itself. Source: https://a16z.com/the-greenfield-strategy-ai-native-startup-bingo/ ; https://a16z.com/notes-on-ai-apps-in-2026/. Why it matters: the "AI wrapper" critique is now a cliché; the working frontier is *what specifically* makes an AI-native co. unkillable.

- **Vertical AI Agents** — Bessemer's "Ten Principles for Building Strong Vertical AI Businesses" (BVP Atlas, 2025) and Aforza's 2026 outlook position vertical agents (industry-specific, domain-tuned, blurring the SaaS/service line) as the dominant AI-app archetype. Gartner: 80% of enterprises will adopt vertical AI agents by 2026. Source: https://www.bvp.com/atlas/part-iv-ten-principles-for-building-strong-vertical-ai-businesses. Pushes against: horizontal-platform-first thinking.

- **Data Flywheels + Process Complexity as Moat** — Across NFX, Bessemer, and Insignia (April 2025), the consensus is that in 2025 the only durable AI moats are (a) closed-loop data flywheels, (b) production-grade process complexity (the gap between demo and reliable product), (c) domain specificity, (d) human-in-the-loop trust. Source: https://review.insignia.vc/2025/04/15/moats-ai/. Why it matters: replaces the lazy "we have proprietary data" claim with a more precise vocabulary about *what kind* of data and *what kind* of loop.

- **Strategy Blocks (Chandra Janakiraman, via Lenny's Newsletter, Feb 2025)** — A five-block operator's framework for product strategy used at Meta, Headspace, VRChat. Becoming a default reference among CPOs. Source: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/strategy-blocks-an-operators-guide. Why it matters: the framework Lenny's audience (PMs, senior ops) is most likely citing right now.

- **JTBD, three-dimensional version** — Jobs-to-be-Done is alive but now framed as three jobs at once (functional + emotional + social). The "demand-side / switch interview" school (Moesta) and the "outcome-driven" school (Ulwick) are increasingly described as complementary rather than rival. Source: https://gopractice.io/product/jobs-to-be-done-the-theory-and-the-frameworks/ (2025). Why it matters: a smart reader will detect anyone still using a flat 2010s JTBD as the whole story.

- **Superagency (Reid Hoffman, Jan 2025)** — Hoffman + Greg Beato's reframing of agentic AI: humans don't lose agency to AI, they get *super*-agency by giving systems goals and self-planning. Source: https://www.superagency.ai/. Pushes against: zero-sum "AI replaces humans" framing.

- **Abundant Intelligence / The Gentle Singularity (Sam Altman, 2025)** — Two blog posts arguing intelligence cost converges toward the cost of electricity; 2025 = agents doing cognitive work, 2026 = novel insights, 2027 = real-world robotics. Source: https://blog.samaltman.com/abundant-intelligence ; https://blog.samaltman.com/the-gentle-singularity. Why it matters: the canonical "intelligence too cheap to meter" framing that frontier essays are responding to (positively or sceptically).

- **Scale-Innovation / Industrialized Innovation (China)** — Dan Wang (book *Breakneck*, 2025) and Kyle Chan (Princeton / Brookings, High Capacity newsletter, 2025) reframe Chinese innovation not as imitation-then-leapfrog but as **dissolving the boundary between innovation and scaling**. Chan's model: overlapping ecosystems (batteries → EVs → energy storage → drones) cross-pollinate; commercialization speed is the real moat, not subsidies. Source: https://restofworld.org/2025/dan-wang-breakneck-book-china-tech/ ; https://www.highcapacity.org/p/chinas-tech-long-game. Why it matters: this is the framing Western readers are *just* surfacing for — and the most useful one for a writer working across the China/West boundary.

## 2. Emerging critiques and contrarian positions

- **"Innovation theater" critique, sharper teeth in 2025** — Capgemini's recurring number (80–90% of corporate innovation labs fail) is now used as a stick to argue that hackathons, demo days, and "leveraging AI" rhetoric are anti-signals. John Brandon Elam, Medium Nov 2025: companies "mistake performance for progress." Bas Wenneker's "6 Signs Your Innovation Studio Is Actually a Theatre" continues to circulate. Source: https://john-elam.medium.com/innovation-theater-when-companies-mistake-performance-for-progress-4de18d3ecdc9. Why it matters: any opening that even *gestures* at corporate-innovation-program language now reads as suspect.

- **"Wrapper apps are dead"** — By mid-2025, the consensus among a16z, NFX, and Euclid Ventures is that thin AI wrappers on top of GPT/Claude get an instant pass — ChatGPT's 800M users can replicate them in-app. The contrarian inside the contrarian: process complexity and integrated workflows *are* the new wrapper, but they're survivable. Source: https://insights.euclid.vc/p/dude-wheres-my-moat. Why it matters: the "wrapper" word is itself a 2024-stale frame; 2025 frontier asks "what's the data flywheel."

- **Christensen Disruption Theory, half-rejected** — TechCrunch ("What if Christensen was wrong about disruption?", Jan 2024) is the canonical recent broadside: iPhone, Tesla, Nvidia were all *more expensive*, not cheaper, than incumbents — the opposite of low-end disruption. Counter-defenders (Christensen Institute, IMD) point to BYD vs. Western OEMs and DeepSeek vs. OpenAI as proof the theory holds *when you look at the right players*. The live debate: is disruption theory still load-bearing or just a vocabulary? Source: https://techcrunch.com/2024/01/30/what-if-christensen-was-wrong-about-disruption/. Why it matters: invoking Christensen uncritically marks the writer as a 2010s reader.

- **Founder-Market Fit > Product-Market Fit (pre-PMF reframe)** — Several 2025 essays (VCCorner, Sajith Pai, Jackyraj on Medium) argue VCs in 2025 are filtering for founder-market fit *before* PMF is even a meaningful question — especially in AI, where the speed of model improvement makes products ephemeral but founder conviction durable. Source: https://www.thevccorner.com/p/founder-market-fit-vc-guide. Why it matters: "PMF first" is the default frame this critique punctures.

- **The Bitter Lesson, contested limits** — Rich Sutton's classic ("general methods + compute beat handcrafted systems") was the dominant 2024 mantra; 2025 saw pushback that messy organizational/workplace objectives don't have the clean reward signals of chess or Go, so scale alone doesn't solve them. See dbreunig (Aug 2025): "Does the Bitter Lesson Have Limits?" ; rlancemartin (Jul 2025): "Learning the Bitter Lesson." Source: https://www.dbreunig.com/2025/08/01/does-the-bitter-lesson-have-limits.html. Why it matters: this is the most generative philosophical argument in the AI/innovation space right now — scale-maximalism vs. context-engineering.

## 3. Vocabulary at the frontier

- **"Agentic engineering"** — Karpathy's successor term to vibe coding. Used by: Karpathy, The New Stack, AI Agents Simplified Substack. Example: "the new default is that you are not writing the code directly 99% of the time, you are orchestrating agents." Source: https://thenewstack.io/vibe-coding-is-passe/

- **"Context engineering"** — Replacing "prompt engineering." Used by: Simon Willison, Hugo Bowne, NxCode, McKinsey QuantumBlack. The shift is from clever single prompts to architecting the entire information ecosystem an agent operates inside. Source: https://hugobowne.substack.com/p/ai-agent-harness-3-principles-for-context-engineering

- **"Data flywheel"** — Used by NFX, Bessemer, Insignia, a16z. Tightly specified: a closed loop where user interactions continuously generate proprietary feedback that improves the product. Distinct from generic "proprietary data." Source: https://review.insignia.vc/2025/04/15/moats-ai/

- **"Three-person unicorn" / "one-person unicorn"** — NFX coined "3-Person Unicorn" (resurfaced 2025 around Cursor, ElevenLabs, Mercor); Pieter Levels is the canonical solo example. Marks the writer as following the AI-native-org conversation. Source: https://www.nfx.com/post/the-3-person-unicorn-startup

- **"Superagency"** — Hoffman/Beato (Jan 2025). Reframes the agent debate from threat-to-humans toward leverage-for-humans. Source: https://www.superagency.ai/

- **"Cascading coordination"** — Sangeet Paul Chowdary, *Reshuffle* (2025). The argument that exponential change is less about compounding and more about one solved coordination problem unlocking the next. A sharper alternative to "compounding" cliché. Source: via "Separating fads from trends..." Only Dead Fish, Aug 2025 — https://onlydeadfish.co.uk/2025/08/03/separating-fads-from-trends-and-the-second-and-third-order-effects-of-ai/

- **"Second-order effects"** — Standard 2025 vocabulary in AI strategy writing (Thoughts from the Data Front, Hyperleap, World Economic Forum). The first-order effects (cost reduction, automation) are conceded; the live game is shifting customer expectations and new business models. Source: https://www.thoughtsfromthedatafront.com/p/ais-second-order-effects-the-business

- **"Scale-innovation" / "China speed" / "dissolving the boundary between innovation and scaling"** — Dan Wang's phrasing, picked up by Kyle Chan, IMD, AMS. The technical term sophisticated readers use instead of "China is copying" or "China is innovating." Source: https://restofworld.org/2025/dan-wang-breakneck-book-china-tech/

- **"Taste" (as a competitive moat)** — Paul Graham (Feb 2025 X post: "In the AI age, taste will become even…"); Julie Zhuo (June 2025 Medium essay with Ivan Zhao); the iontelligence "Taste Economy" framing. The argument: when AI flattens execution, taste is what's left. Source: https://joulee.medium.com/when-ai-has-better-taste-than-you-0dc8e870db9a ; https://iontelligence.substack.com/p/issue-70-ai-has-created-a-taste-economy

- **"Evals"** — Now a default noun in serious AI-product writing (Anthropic, LangChain "State of Agent Engineering," OpenAI dev blog 2025). "Evals, graders, and tuning" replaced "prompt engineering" as the discipline mentioned in production-AI essays. Source: https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/demystifying-evals-for-ai-agents

- **"AI-native vs. retrofit"** — a16z's preferred binary (2025–2026). Marks any writer treating AI as a feature rather than a substrate as out of the conversation. Source: https://a16z.com/the-greenfield-strategy-ai-native-startup-bingo/
